# Character Design Prompts for "Prometheus' Wrath"

## Character 1: John Chen

### Basic Profile
- **Name**: John Chen
- **Gender/Age**: Male, 25 years old
- **Identity/Role**: Class-E Test Subject, Spaceship Custodian
- **Personality Tags**: Resilient, Curious, Coexisting Inferiority and Rebellion, Hidden Intellectual Potential

### Visual Character Description

#### Basic Appearance
A 25-year-old Asian male with an elongated face, defined but sunken jawline, and slightly protruding cheekbones.
Dark brown almond-shaped eyes with slightly drooping outer corners, his gaze carrying constant confusion and alertness, eyelids slightly swollen from chronic fatigue.
Straight nasal bridge, slightly pointed nose tip. Thin lips, pale lip color, habitual tight-lipped expression.
Thick eyebrows with pronounced brow ridge, typically slightly furrowed.
Pale sallow complexion, lacking sunlight exposure over long periods.
Black short hair, somewhat messy, with longer front bangs partially covering eyebrows, hair lying flat against the temples, dry and brittle texture.
175cm height, thin build, narrow shoulders, slightly hunched posture (inferiority complex posture).

#### Defining Features
- Obvious electronic interface at the back of the left side of the head (brain-computer interface, gray metallic sheen)
- Glowing electronic tattoo on the back of the left hand (simple geometric pattern, blue glow)
- Implanted data interface at the back of the neck (circular metal cover plate)

#### Costume
Dark gray jumpsuit uniform, coarse synthetic fiber material with obvious wear marks.
Worn identification badge on the chest, collar slightly open.
Tool belt around the waist (containing cleaning tools), simple data terminal on the wrist.
Heavy work boots, stained with grime.

#### Temperament/Style
Defeated yet resilient bottom-layer survivor, gaze carrying confusion and alertness, hidden spark of brilliance.

## Character Application Guidelines

### Application in Nano Banana Image Generation
1. **Character Subject**: Use the English natural language prompts provided above
2. **Feature Locking**: Repeat defining features (such as quantum scar, mechanical eye) in every description
3. **Consistency Maintenance**: Use the same feature descriptions whenever the same character appears
4. **Emotional Variation**: Adjust character state through expression and action vocabulary

### Application in Seedance 2.0 Video Generation
1. **Unified Reference**: Specify characters using fixed features, such as "that 25-year-old Asian male with the electronic interface at the back of his head"
2. **Avoid Confusion**: Use different defining features to distinguish between characters
3. **Action Description**: Design shots combining character-specific action descriptions

### Reference Image Generation Suggestions
- Generate 5-angle reference images for each main character
- Angles: Front, 45-degree left, 90-degree left, Close-up, Full body
- After generating reference images using Nano Banana prompts, use them for character consistency locking in subsequent video generation

### Core Writing Principles

#### 1. Write Like Telling a Story
- Describe scenes naturally and coherently
- Include subject, action, and environment in flowing narrative
- ✅ **Example**: "A detective in a trench coat stands under a flickering streetlamp, rain soaking his shoulders. In the background, the neon sign of a desolate bar reflects in a puddle."

#### 2. Include Photography/Cinematography Terminology
- For realistic images, specify camera angle, lens type, lighting setup
- For comic/storyboard, specify panel composition, art style
- ✅ **Example**: "Captured with an 85mm portrait lens, resulting in soft bokeh"

#### 3. Specify Technical Parameters
- Always include aspect ratio (vertical/landscape/square)
- Include resolution requirements when needed
- ✅ **Example**: "Vertical portrait orientation" or "Square image"

#### 4. Model-Specific Optimizations

**For Nano Banana (Gemini 2.5 Flash Image)**:
- Use concise prompts, avoid over-complexity
- Focus on core subject + setting + lighting
- Best for: Quick generation, sticker designs, basic realism

**For Nano Banana Pro (Gemini 3 Pro Image)**:
- Can handle more complex multi-character scenes
- Better at detailed text rendering
- Best for: Professional-grade commercial photography, complex compositions

**For Nano Banana 2 (Gemini 3.1 Flash Image)**:
- Best overall balance of speed and quality
- Excellent Chinese character support
- Can maintain consistency for up to 5 characters and 14 objects
- Best for: 4K output, complex storyboards, professional workflows

### Six Official Template Styles

#### 1. Realistic Photography
**Template**: "A photorealistic [shot type] of [subject], [action or expression], set in [environment]. The scene is illuminated by [lighting description], creating a [mood] atmosphere. Captured with a [camera/lens details], emphasizing [key textures and details]. The image should be in a [aspect ratio] format."

#### 2. Stickers and Illustrations
**Template**: "A [style] sticker of a [subject], featuring [key characteristics] and a [color palette]. The design should have [line style] and [shading style]. The background must be white."

#### 3. Text Rendering
**Template**: "Create a [image type] for [brand/concept] with the text '[text to render]' in a [font style]. The design should be [style description], with a [color scheme]."

#### 4. Commercial Product Photography
**Template**: "A high-resolution, studio-lit product photograph of [product description] on [background surface]. The lighting is [lighting setup] to [lighting purpose]. The camera angle is [angle type] to showcase [specific feature]. Ultra-realistic, with sharp focus on [key detail]. [Aspect ratio]."

#### 5. Minimalist/Whitespace Design
**Template**: "A minimalist composition featuring a single [subject] positioned in the [position] of the frame. The background is a vast, empty [color] canvas, creating significant negative space. Soft, subtle lighting. [Aspect ratio]."

#### 6. Comics/Storyboard
**Template**: "A single comic book panel in a [art style] style. In the foreground, [character description and action]. In the background, [setting details]. The panel has a [dialogue/caption box] with the text '[Text]'. The lighting creates a [mood] mood. [Aspect ratio]."
